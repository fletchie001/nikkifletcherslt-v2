Supervision

Time to stop, look up, and reflect

What is Supervision?

Professional Clinical Supervision

"Every therapist needs time to stop, look up from their daily work and reflect — taking time out can enable us to renew our energy and bring a greater clarity to your work."

Shohet & Shohet, 2020

Supervision is "an essential part of good professional practice" (CQC, 2013) and "the formal arrangement that enables you to discuss your work regularly with someone who is experienced and qualified" (RCSLT, 2013).

I offer high-quality, regular Professional Clinical Supervision for therapists and assistants.

Experience

What I bring to supervision

  • I have worked for 20 years as a paediatric speech and language therapist, 10 years in teams within the NHS and 10 years working independently as a sole trader.
  • I have supervised student SLTs, multi-therapy assistants and therapists including newly qualified.
  • I am passionate about developing therapists and supporting our profession to grow and develop.
  • I provide a safe, non-judgmental space for reflection, support, learning, and development, committed to culturally responsive and anti-racist practices.
  • I have completed Levels 1–3 Supervision Training with 'Intandem'.
  • Supervision follows the evidence-based guidelines provided by the RCSLT.
  • I prioritise my own supervision in line with HCPC and RCSLT Guidelines.
  • I have knowledge and experience with the RCSLT Professional Development Framework to support reflection on your development and career progression.
Process

What to expect

  • A free 20-minute introductory phone call will help you decide if this is the right supervision arrangement for you.
  • We will set up an agreement to establish what our supervision relationship will look like and review this frequently to make sure it's right for you.
  • Supervision takes place online via Zoom or Teams, making it flexible and accessible wherever you are based.

If you are looking for regular supervision, do get in touch.
